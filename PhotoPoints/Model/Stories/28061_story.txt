Birds including catbirds, grosbeaks, juncos, thrushes, bluebirds, chickadees, flickers, finches, quail, and grouse are known to eat the seeds. Swallowtail, common wood nymph, and other butterflies harvest the nectar. Deer and elk browse the shrub.

This shrub has showy and aromatic flowers, and it has horticultural potential in native plant gardens.
