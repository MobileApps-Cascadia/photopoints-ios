Grosbeaks, woodpeckers, grouse, band-tailed pigeons, mourning doves, jays, robins, and tanagers eat berries. It also attracts many insectivorous birds, including bushtits, kinglets, chickadees, flycatchers, and nuthatches. Swallowtail, gray hairstreak, and other butterfly larvae eat leaves and other plants. Black bears, foxes, coyotes, and raccoons eat the fruit.

They're found in marshes or swamps, near stream or river banks, bottomlands, coniferous forests, mixed forests, forest edges, thickets, and logged sites.
